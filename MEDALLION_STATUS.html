Medallion Status

Last updated: 2026-03-06 • Version 1.4

Overview

Medallion Status is a prestige and recognition program that rewards competitive play, tournament participation, community involvement, and fair conduct. Status may grant cosmetic badges, early feature access, queue priority, and guild privileges — including the ability for Guildmasters to restrict their guild to players of a minimum tier.

System-wide Status

Medallion Status is a single platform-wide rank. Prestige points earned in any Ken's Games title contribute to the same status pool. A player who reaches Gold through FastTrack will appear as Gold in BrickBreaker3D, the lobby, guild listings, and every other part of the platform — without needing to separately earn status in each game. Your highest earned tier is always displayed everywhere.

Tiers

  • Bronze: Starting tier for all new members; baseline benefits.
  • Silver: Established participation; occasional early access to new content.
  • Gold: High participation or skill; priority access and cosmetic rewards.
  • Platinum: Top contributors and competitors; extended privileges.
  • Diamond: Elite tier; peak prestige, exclusive recognition, and maximum platform privileges.
  • Founders (invite-only): Legacy recognition for founding members; special badge.

The Schwarz Diamond Lattice

The prestige point framework across all Ken's Games is called the Schwarz Diamond Lattice. Like the mathematical Schwarz Diamond — a triply periodic minimal surface whose primitive is the saddle z = xy, tiled at 90° angles to form an infinite interlocking lattice — the prestige system forms a unified, cross-game structure: every game connects into the same lattice, every point earned anywhere strengthens the same whole, and the entire structure builds toward Diamond, the apex tier.

The lattice has two layers that combine into every player's single system-wide prestige total:

Core Layer — Shared by All Games

The following actions award prestige points regardless of which game you are playing. These form the foundation nodes of the lattice:

  • Completing a ranked match (win or loss)
  • Winning a ranked match
  • Achieving a personal best score or record
  • Participating in a guild tournament
  • Placing in a guild tournament (top half, finalist, or winner tiers each award more)
  • Maintaining a fair-play record over a season
  • Community contributions: bug reports, UX feedback, mentoring, localization work

Game Layer — Added to the Lattice per Title

Each game extends the lattice with its own prestige point actions unique to that title. These points add directly to the same platform-wide total. Examples:

  • FastTrack: Completing a game without losing a peg; winning a multi-player lobby match; achieving a card-combo milestone.
  • BrickBreaker3D: Clearing an entire sector's bricks; surviving all rounds without losing sphere integrity; top scorer in a multi-player arena session.

Game-specific point definitions are maintained in each game's individual rules page and may be updated as games evolve. New games joining the platform add their own layer to the lattice without changing the core.

Earning and Maintaining Status

  • Prestige points from all games and all categories accumulate into one platform total.
  • Reaching the point threshold for the next tier automatically advances your Medallion Status.
  • Integrity: Cheating, harassment, or policy violations may deduct prestige points or reduce your status tier.
  • Decay: Status can decay after prolonged inactivity across all games; reminders are sent before any downgrade.

Benefits Matrix (examples)

  • Badge + profile highlight in chat and lobbies
  • Early/preview access to new maps/modes
  • Queue priority for premium servers during peak hours
  • Guild creation/voting privileges at higher tiers
  • Cosmetic unlocks and seasonal challenges

Review and Appeals

Status changes are automated with periodic manual review to ensure fairness. You may appeal downgrades or submit supporting evidence via support.

Transparency

Aggregated statistics about medallion distribution may be published to maintain fairness and community trust.
