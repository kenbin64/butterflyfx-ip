Guild Rules

Last updated: 2026-03-06 • Version 1.1

Purpose

Guilds are player-led communities. These rules protect a welcoming, safe, and creative environment while supporting competitive play.

Guild Scope

Guilds may be created as one of two types:

  • System-wide Guilds: Open to all players across Ken's Games regardless of which game they primarily play. Members may represent the guild in any supported game on the platform. System-wide guilds appear on the platform-level leaderboard and are eligible to organize cross-game tournaments.
  • Game-specific Guilds: Tied to a single game (e.g., FastTrack, BrickBreaker3D). Membership, rankings, and tournament eligibility are scoped to that game only. Game-specific guilds appear on that game's leaderboard.

Guild type is set at creation and cannot be changed afterward. A player may belong to one system-wide guild and one game-specific guild per game simultaneously.

Guild Creation and Leadership

  • Any registered player may create a guild and automatically becomes its Guildmaster (GM).
  • The Guildmaster has absolute authority over their guild: membership approval, moderation, suspensions, bans, and guild chat.
  • If the Guildmaster quits or abandons the guild, the guild is immediately dissolved. There is no automatic succession.
  • Guildmaster Transfer: A Guildmaster may designate a successor only with the written approval of both the current Guildmaster and the platform Superuser. Only the Superuser can execute the transfer. Without this dual approval, no transfer occurs and the guild dissolves on the Guildmaster's departure.

Guild Chat

  • Chat is an optional feature that Guildmasters may enable for their guild.
  • When enabled, chat is available exclusively to current guild members.
  • Guildmasters may use guild chat to communicate with members and to coordinate with other Guildmasters to organize tournaments.
  • A Guild Search tool is available to Guildmasters to discover and contact other guilds for tournament organization.

Membership and Moderation

  • Guildmasters approve all membership requests. Joining a guild requires explicit Guildmaster approval.
  • Guildmasters may remove members at any time by suspension (fixed duration) or ban (permanent).
  • Guildmasters may restrict their guild to players who meet a minimum Medallion Status tier. Players who fall below the required tier have the same appeal rights as suspended members.
  • Matchmaker games organized through a guild may require a minimum skill or Medallion level as set by the Guildmaster.

Appeals within a Guild

  • Suspended or banned members may submit an appeal directly to their Guildmaster. Appeals do not go to platform staff or the Superuser.
  • Appeal accepted: The member is immediately reinstated.
  • Appeal denied: The member must serve the full suspension period. Permanent bans may only ever be lifted by the Guildmaster — no other party can override this.
  • 24-hour re-action cooldown: After any reinstatement (including via accepted appeal), the Guildmaster must wait 24 hours before issuing a new ban or suspension against that same member.

Player-to-Player Bans (General Bans)

  • Any player may ban any other player on the platform. No notice is given and there is no appeal process.
  • The effect is mutual invisibility: the two players will not see each other on the platform and will never be paired together in games.
  • If both players are in the same guild, they cannot see each other's presence in the guild or chat with each other.
  • Guild member protection: A player may not issue a general ban against another player while they share the same guild. To ban a fellow guild member, a player must first leave the guild, then issue the ban.

Guild-to-Guild Bans

  • A Guildmaster may ban another guild from their guild's perspective. This action is unannounced and unappealable.
  • Once banned, the target guild and all its members will no longer see the banning guild in searches or anywhere on the platform, as if it does not exist.

Superuser

  • There is one platform Superuser (Kenneth Bingham). The Superuser role cannot be granted to any other player.
  • The Superuser may ban any player on the platform, dissolve any guild, and is the only party who can execute a Guildmaster transfer.
  • Superuser bans carry the same appeal rights as any other action: the affected party may contact the Superuser directly to appeal.

Code of Conduct

  • Be respectful. No harassment, hate speech, or targeted abuse.
  • Play fair. No cheating, exploiting, or unauthorized automation.
  • Keep it appropriate. No NSFW content in public spaces.
  • Protect privacy. Do not dox or share private information.
  • Follow the law. No illegal content or activity.

Content Policy

  • Original content only or content used with permission.
  • Mark spoilers and sensitive content.
  • Credit creators; respect licenses.

Tournaments

Tournaments are organized, multi-guild competitive events approved and announced by participating Guildmasters.

Organization

  • A Guildmaster proposes a tournament; participating Guildmasters must agree before play begins.
  • Approved tournaments are announced to all Guildmasters and promoted in the game lobby, allowing general players to request joining a guild or forming one in order to participate.
  • Tournament terms (format, eligibility, scoring) must be published before sign-up closes.
  • System-wide guilds may organize cross-game tournaments; game-specific guilds may only organize tournaments within their game.

Formats

  • Elimination: Single or double elimination bracket. Losing a match (single) or two matches (double) eliminates a team or guild from the tournament.
  • Round Robin: All participants play each other; standings are determined by cumulative score or win record across all rounds.
  • Hybrid formats are permitted if agreed upon by all participating Guildmasters upfront.

Conduct

  • All Guild Rules and the Code of Conduct apply in full during tournament play.
  • Match manipulation, collusion, or deliberate forfeiting to benefit another guild is grounds for disqualification and Medallion Status review.

Results and Rewards

  • Guild tournament winners are recognized on platform and game leaderboards.
  • Merely participating in a tournament earns prestige points toward Medallion Status advancement. Winning earns significantly more.

Integration with Medallion Status

  • Tournament participation and wins earn Medallion prestige points (Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond).
  • Higher Medallion tiers may unlock additional guild and moderation privileges.
  • Severe policy violations may result in Medallion Status reduction.

Changes

Rules may be updated for clarity or safety. Continued participation indicates acceptance of updates.
