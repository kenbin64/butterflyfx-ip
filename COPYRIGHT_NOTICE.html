Mathematical Attributions

Certain visual and structural designs used in ButterflyFX games and platform systems are inspired by or derived from mathematical structures in the public domain. These are acknowledged here:

  • Schwarz D-Surface (Diamond Minimal Surface) — discovered by Carl Hermann Amandus Schwarz (1843–1921). The Schwarz Diamond is a triply periodic minimal surface with diamond cubic symmetry. Its primitive form is the saddle surface defined by z = xy — a hyperbolic paraboloid. The full surface is constructed by tiling copies of this primitive joined at 90° angles, producing the characteristic interlocking lattice.
  • Structural properties of the Schwarz D-Surface: As a minimal surface, it achieves the minimum possible material for the maximum enclosed area — the least surface spans the greatest volume. The geometry is self-supporting: every point in the lattice bears and distributes load to the points above and around it, producing maximum structural support from minimum mass. Every node simultaneously represents every angle of curvature — no direction is privileged, making the structure isotropic: equally strong and equally curved in all orientations. Every inflection point on the primitive saddle is reflected and continued across the joining boundaries, so that the complete lattice resolves into a continuous network of interlocking helices — each helix winding through the structure and connecting to others at every node. This combination — minimal material, maximum area, omnidirectional support, and a lattice of helices — is the geometric and structural inspiration for BrickBreaker3D's crystalline brick arrangement and for the Schwarz Diamond Lattice prestige framework, in which every contribution connects to and supports the whole from any direction.
  • This structure is used as the geometric and conceptual basis for BrickBreaker3D's crystalline brick lattice and for the Schwarz Diamond Lattice prestige point framework across Ken's Games. The mathematical surface itself is in the public domain. The three-dimensional model and artwork depicted in ButterflyFX materials was created as an original work by Kenneth Bingham in Blender, using the Blender Math Function surface tool to generate the z = xy primitive and manually assembling the individual pieces at 90° angles to form the complete lattice. This original 3D artwork, its arrangement, and all derivative visual applications within Ken's Games are proprietary to Kenneth Bingham — all rights reserved.

Proprietary Concepts Derived from the Schwarz D-Surface

The following original concepts were developed by Kenneth Bingham and are proprietary. They are inspired by the mathematical properties of the Schwarz D-Surface but constitute independent intellectual works beyond the public domain mathematics:

  • Schwarz Diamond Substrate — Universal Data Generation: Because every node of the Schwarz Diamond simultaneously contains every angle of curvature and every inflection point is continuously reflected through the lattice, the structure possesses sufficient degrees of freedom to represent any data type. This gives rise to the concept of the Schwarz Diamond Substrate: a generative foundation from which any data structure can be derived on demand, without pre-storage or prefabricated data. No data is stored in advance. Instead, the substrate — defined entirely by its mathematical properties — produces the required structure at the moment it is needed. The substrate itself is the definition; instantiation replaces retrieval. This concept and its application to computing, game systems, and data architecture are original works of Kenneth Bingham — all rights reserved.
