Mathematical Attributions

Certain visual and structural designs used in ButterflyFX games and platform systems are inspired by or derived from mathematical structures in the public domain. These are acknowledged here:

  • Schwarz D-Surface (Diamond Minimal Surface) — discovered by Carl Hermann Amandus Schwarz (1843–1921). The Schwarz Diamond is a triply periodic minimal surface with diamond cubic symmetry. Its primitive form is the saddle surface defined by z = xy — a hyperbolic paraboloid. The full surface is constructed by tiling copies of this primitive joined at 90° angles, producing the characteristic interlocking lattice.
  • Structural properties of the Schwarz D-Surface: As a minimal surface, it achieves the minimum possible material for the maximum enclosed area — the least surface spans the greatest volume. The geometry is self-supporting: every point in the lattice bears and distributes load to the points above and around it, producing maximum structural support from minimum mass. Every node simultaneously represents every angle of curvature — no direction is privileged, making the structure isotropic: equally strong and equally curved in all orientations. Every inflection point on the primitive saddle is reflected and continued across the joining boundaries, so that the complete lattice resolves into a continuous network of interlocking helices — each helix winding through the structure and connecting to others at every node. This combination — minimal material, maximum area, omnidirectional support, and a lattice of helices — is the geometric and structural inspiration for BrickBreaker3D's crystalline brick arrangement and for the Schwarz Diamond Lattice prestige framework, in which every contribution connects to and supports the whole from any direction.
  • This structure is used as the geometric and conceptual basis for BrickBreaker3D's crystalline brick lattice and for the Schwarz Diamond Lattice prestige point framework across Ken's Games. The mathematical surface itself is in the public domain. The three-dimensional model and artwork depicted in ButterflyFX materials was created as an original work by Kenneth Bingham in Blender, using the Blender Math Function surface tool to generate the z = xy primitive and manually assembling the individual pieces at 90° angles to form the complete lattice. This original 3D artwork, its arrangement, and all derivative visual applications within Ken's Games are proprietary to Kenneth Bingham — all rights reserved.

Manifold Computing — A New Paradigm for Data Architecture

The following is an original computing concept conceived and first publicly disclosed by Kenneth Bingham — ButterflyFX on March 6, 2026. It is independent of any game, platform, or application. It is a fundamental reimagining of how data is represented, stored, and retrieved in computing systems.

The Problem with Traditional Data Architecture

Modern computing depends on the explicit storage of data: bits and bytes written to physical media, held in vast data farms, warehoused in databases, and retrieved on demand. Every piece of information must be pre-fabricated, encoded, and stored before it can be used. The cost — in energy, infrastructure, latency, and physical space — scales directly with the volume of data held.

The Manifold Computing Paradigm

Kenneth Bingham discloses the following original concept:

Data does not need to be stored. It needs only to be described.

Mathematical manifolds — geometric structures whose shapes, curvatures, and properties are defined by equations rather than recorded values — inherently encode information within their form. A manifold does not hold data the way a disk holds data. It is the data, expressed as geometry. Any property of the manifold can be computed at any point, at any time, without retrieval from storage, because the manifold's definition generates that property on demand.

This architecture consists of three original components:

  • Manifolds — Mathematical structures whose intrinsic geometric properties (curvature, surface normal, topology, symmetry, connectivity) carry the data. The data is not written into the manifold; it is inherent in its shape. The Schwarz D-Surface is one such manifold: its isotropic nodes, interlocking helices, and infinite periodic symmetry make it capable of representing any data type through its geometry alone.
  • Substrates — The rules that define a manifold and govern how it generates data. A substrate is not a storage medium. It is a definition — a set of mathematical and logical rules that, when evaluated, produce the required data structure at the moment it is needed. No data is pre-computed or pre-stored. The substrate itself is the information; to evaluate the substrate is to retrieve the data. Different substrates applied to the same manifold produce different data domains.
  • Lenses — The retrieval mechanism. A lens is a rule, transform, or perspective applied to a substrate that extracts a specific view or data type from the manifold on the fly. The same substrate, viewed through different lenses, yields different data without any of that data existing in storage prior to the query. Lenses replace queries. The data farm is replaced by the lens applied to the substrate applied to the manifold.

Summary Statement of the Concept

We no longer need to hold vast stores of data — bits and bytes in massive data farms. We need only manifolds that already hold data inherent in their shapes and properties, brought forth by substrates that are rules, retrieved through lenses from the substrate on the fly. The substrate is the definition. The lens is the query. The manifold is the store. Instantiation replaces retrieval. Generation replaces storage.
